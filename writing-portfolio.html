Celeste – How a Platformer Does Plot

Celeste was one of the biggest indie titles of 2018. It was beloved for its easy-to-grasp but difficult-to-master platforming mechanics. Combine that with its colourful pixel-art and its skilfully-made soundtrack, it’s no wonder that players and critics alike fell in love with the title.

Yet the story and writing are what jumped out to me, as it was unlike any other platformer I’d ever played. It wasn’t a set of levels strung together by the excuse of having a princess to save. Nor was the plot hidden in the background, revealed only through cutscenes, as your character remained oblivious and unaffected by the story.

Instead, Celeste offered glimpses into real characters with real struggles. The player was encouraged to empathize with them through both dialogue and gameplay. Making it all the more satisfying to see the main character grow and overcome their struggles.

It’s this kind of storytelling that should be more common in videogames, and I think there are a few lessons that the industry’s writers and designers can learn from Celeste.

Blending Plot and Gameplay

Perhaps the most powerful of these lessons is to blend the plot and gameplay. This helps the player relate to their character as they’ll both share the same experience, in the same manner, with the same feelings. This immerses the player into the plot and makes them care for their character.

Celeste does this early on when the main character first meets her doppelganger and is instilled with fear. The game helps you relate to her fear by speeding up and demanding that you run away. Your own heart beats faster and faster as you try to avoid being caught and restarting the level. By the time you escape the doppelganger and reach safety, you’re in tune with the main character and feel the same sense of relief.

Making the Story Personal

Even if the game blends its plot and gameplay, it can still be hard to relate to the characters and events. That’s because the vast majority of games focus on external threats, with aliens, zombies, or corrupt organizations making up your typical opponent. It’s also always the fate of the world that’s at stake, and it can be hard for us to relate to something so grand.

That’s why more personal stories are needed in this medium, as they give a character the same struggles many of us face in our daily lives whether it’s dealing with the loss of a loved one, facing mental illness, or our struggles in becoming better versions of ourselves.

Celeste demonstrates this by focusing the story on the main character’s depression. The whole story is centred around her coming face to face with her mental illness, accepting it, and learning to overcome it. This is no doubt similar to the struggles felt by the game’s writer and many of its players.

Using Metaphors

While metaphors aren’t required to make a good story, they’re still a powerful tool that often goes underutilized in the videogame industry. They can give a story more depth and nuance, without requiring much more effort on the writer’s part. Metaphors can also give the player a sense of pride as they’ve uncovered the story’s hidden meaning.

Unfortunately, it often feels like videogame writers don’t trust the player’s intelligence, as they feel the need to spell out every little detail instead of leaving some parts to be inferred. While this definitely isn’t the case in all games, it’s quite common in mainstream titles who, in their defence, are trying to go for a broad target audience.

We can see Celeste successfully using metaphors on multiple occasions. The doppelganger that appears in the story is a clear representation of the main character’s depression and insecurity. The mountain being climbed is also a metaphor, and it represents the main characters internal struggles. Upon connecting these dots and understanding what’s really going on, the player feels more invested in Celeste since they played a vital role in uncovering the secrets of the story.


By learning to blend plot and gameplay, writing more personal stories, and using metaphors as part of their writing toolbox, videogame writers can push the medium to new heights. They’ll be able to create stories that touch the heart, make you think, and that are truly unforgettable.

Stardew Valley Tuna | Where to Find, How to Catch

Tuna is a delicious fish, both in real life and in video games. In Stardew Valley, it’s used for a number of recipes including fish tacos and sashimi. It can even be used to make quality fertilizer and the ocean fish bundle. So, if you’re having trouble finding Stardew Valley tuna, you came to the right place.

Where to Find Tuna in Stardew Valley?

If you think finding tuna is going to be a massive grind in one tiny part of the map, then shed away your fears, because catching tuna is simple and straightforward.

To find the fish, simply head south of Pelican Town and down to the ocean and begin fishing. A good place to set up shop is by Willy’s Fish Shop, so you can get more supplies if you need them.

However, keep the seasons in mind when deciding to go hunting for tuna, as the fish can only be caught in the summer and winter. Also you’ll want to be sure to go during the day, since the fish can’t be caught at night — so no midnight fishing trips.

Surprisingly enough, that isn’t the only place to find tuna in Stardew Valley, as there’s a small chance of it spawning in garbage cans during summer and winter. Feel free to go dumpster diving if you fancy your luck.

How to Catch Tuna in Stardew Valley?

Catching tuna is just like catching any other fish in Stardew Valley. All you have to do is equip a fishing rod and throw the line out into the water. Then just wait until a fish bites — indicated by an exclamation mark and the game saying “HIT!” — then just click to start the mini-game.

Now here’s the tricky part, to beat the mini-game and catch the fish, you’ll have to keep a rectangle near the moving fish icon until a bar fills up completely. It sounds easier than it is, but after practicing it once or twice, you’ll get the hang of it.

Now that you know where and how to catch tuna in Stardew Valley, so head on out and reel in those fish.
